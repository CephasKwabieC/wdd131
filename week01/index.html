Accra - Ghana

Accra is the capital city of Ghana, located on the Gulf of Guinea. It is known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and diverse population. The city serves as the political, economic, and educational center of the country.

Accra is home to several landmarks, including the Kwame Nkrumah Mausoleum, Independence Arch, and the bustling Makola Market. The city also boasts beautiful beaches, such as Labadi Beach and Teshie Beach, which are popular among locals and tourists alike.

Ghana's flag, which features red, yellow, and green stripes with a black star in the center, symbolizes the country's struggle for independence and its commitment to freedom and unity.
